What are Waves?

Waves are disturbances that transfer energy from place to place. They are created when a source of energy causes a medium to vibrate.

Types of Waves

  • Mechanical Waves: These waves require a medium to travel. Examples include sound waves and waves in a spring.
  • Electromagnetic Waves: These waves can travel through the vacuum of space. Examples include light waves and radio waves.
  • Surface Waves: These waves travel along the surface separating two media. An example is waves on the surface of water.

Properties of Waves

  • Amplitude: This is the maximum displacement of points on a wave.
  • Frequency: This is the number of waves that pass a point in a given amount of time.
  • Speed: This is how fast a wave travels.
